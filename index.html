What is the EBIT?
The earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT) is a measurement of the company's profitability that excludes the interest and income tax expenses.
How to calculate EBIT?
To calculate EBIT you should use the following formula:
earnings before interest and tax = revenue - cost of goods sold (COGS) - operating expenses
Example: company x has a revenue of $640,000, $220,000 worth of cost of goods sold (COGS) and $40,000 in operating expenses, calculate the EBIT.
Using the EBIT formula the answer will be:
earnings before interest and tax = revenue - cost of goods sold - operating expenses
earnings before interest and tax = $640,000 - $220,000 - $40,000
earnings before interest and tax = $380,000
Why use EBIT?
EBIT is used to provide a measurement of a company's profitability from operations because it doesn't account for the expenses associated with taxes and interest.
